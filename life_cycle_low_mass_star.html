Life Cycle of a Low-Mass Star

From Nebula to White Dwarf

1. Nebula – Where Stars Are Born

A nebula is a large cloud of gas and dust in space. Gravity pulls the material together to form a star. Nebulae can span hundreds of light-years and are found throughout galaxies.

Example: Orion Nebula (about 1,344 light-years away)

2. Protostar – A Star Is Starting

The gas clump gets hotter and denser. It shines from heat but doesn’t do fusion yet. Protostars can last a few million years before becoming a full star.

3. Main Sequence – Long Life Phase

The star starts nuclear fusion—changing hydrogen into helium. It stays in this stable stage for billions of years. Our Sun is in this phase and is about 4.6 billion years old.

Example: The Sun (diameter: about 1.39 million km)

4. Red Giant – Star Grows Bigger

The hydrogen runs out. The core shrinks, outer layers expand, and the star becomes cooler and red. Red giants can be hundreds of times larger than the Sun.

Example: Aldebaran (65 light-years away)

5. Planetary Nebula – Gas Shell Ejected

The star sheds its outer layers, forming a glowing gas cloud. This phase lasts a short time, only about 10,000 years.

Example: Helix Nebula

6. White Dwarf – Star’s Final Form

The remaining core becomes a white dwarf. It’s very hot, dense, and about the size of Earth. Over time, it cools and fades.

Example: Sirius B

    • a. A black hole
    • b. A planet
    • c. A cloud of gas and dust
    • d. A comet
    Answer: c
  2. What process happens in a main sequence star?
    • a. Hydrogen fuses into helium
    • b. Stars explode
    • c. Gas freezes
    • d. The core turns into iron
    Answer: a
  3. What happens when a star becomes a red giant?
    • a. It shrinks
    • b. It disappears
    • c. It explodes
    • d. It expands and cools
    Answer: d
  4. How big is the Sun compared to Earth?
    • a. About 10 times bigger
    • b. About 100 times wider
    • c. The same size
    • d. Smaller
    Answer: b
  5. What is left after a planetary nebula?
    • a. Black hole
    • b. Red giant
    • c. White dwarf
    • d. Main sequence star
    Answer: c
